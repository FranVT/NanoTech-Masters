\chapter*{Abstract}

\cor{This thesis consists of the design of a numerical protocol to simulate the mechanical response of polymeric gels under shear deformation where monomers and crosslinkers are modeled as patchy particles. 
The proposed protocol is closely based on a previously proposed model for PNIPAM microgels. 
The new method allowed the percolation of a patchy particle network at a temperature of \num{0.05}$[T^*]$, with a packing fraction of \num{0.5} and a damp of \num{0.1}$[1/t^*]$ for systems with 3\%, 5\%, and 10\% of crosslinker concentration.
The mechanical response from shear deformation in the $xy$ plane at high strain values $\gamma^*=15$ indicates a viscoelastic response with the characteristic overshoot for polymeric networks.
However, the stress peak of the network is achieved at lower strains in contrast to those reported in the literature.
On the other hand, the fitting of steady stress after the overshoot with the Hershey-Bulckey indicates that the system presents shear thinning and no yield stress; therefore, it is more adequate to refer to it as a viscoelastic fluid than a viscoelastic solid.
These results allow the validation of the methodology as a numerical tool to explore in more detail the molecular mechanisms that contribute significantly to the mechanical response of polymeric gels.
}
